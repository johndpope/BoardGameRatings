It covers the development of ancient civilizations from the invention of agriculture c. 8000 B.C. to the emergence of Rome around the middle of the third century B.C. Each player leads a nation of peoples over a map board of the Eastern Mediterranean and Near East as they attempt to carve a niche for themselves and their culture.&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;Although battles and territorial strategy are important, this is not a war game because it is not won by battle or conquest. Nomad and farmer, warrior and merchant, artisan and citizen all have an essential part to play in the development of civilization.
