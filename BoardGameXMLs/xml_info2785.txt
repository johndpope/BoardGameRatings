This game is much easier than cards game, because it's always wholly displayed to the players, and, being in the form of a snail or spirally folded snake, it's appropriate to print the things that one wants to learn...'.&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;The game became an instant hit in France in the 1600s, and engendered a lot of variants (educational, commemorative, ...) up to the present day.&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;It turned up later in England about 1750, according to Whitehouse, under the title &amp;quot;Royall &amp;amp; most pleasant game of the goose - Invented at the Consistory in Rome and are printed and sold by H. Beach of New York who entitled it The Jolly Game of Goose.
