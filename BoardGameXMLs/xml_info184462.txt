Whoever can get the most gold at the least cost is the winner.&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;In more detail, Alchemidus is a speed puzzle, with players trying to be the fastest to find the cheapest way to convert clay to gold.&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;The game has four pairs of d6 dice, in gold, lead, clay and white to show the process. The 2 gold and clay dice, and 1 of the lead dice are rolled and placed on their respective marker cards. The players must figure how to progress from the two clay numbers, to the one lead number, and then back to the two gold numbers.&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;To do this, there are three rows of five cards dealt out from three small decks. The third row is to modify dice values. The spells are varied and often symmetrical, so you might have one spell saying &amp;quot;If the total is &amp;gt;=7, do...&amp;quot; and another saying &amp;quot;If the total is &amp;lt;=7, do...&amp;quot;, or a spell for odd numbered dice, and another for even numbers.&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;Each spell card has a number on it, the cost of using that spell.
